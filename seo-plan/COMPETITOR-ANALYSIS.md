# Competitor Analysis — pouyakarimi.ir

_Prepared: 2026-06-18. Note: figures below are directional estimates for planning, not live API pulls. Validate with Ahrefs/GSC before committing budget._

## Competitive Frame

A personal freelance/consulting brand doesn't compete with one set of rivals — it competes in **three different arenas** at once, each with different players:

1. **SERP competitors** — who ranks for the service keywords, per locale.
2. **Platform competitors** — Upwork, Toptal, Freelancer, LinkedIn profiles that outrank personal sites for "hire freelance X."
3. **Entity competitors** — other people named or positioned similarly, for the brand/entity space.

## Arena 1 — English service SERPs

| Competitor type | Examples | Strength | Weakness / our angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Established freelance dev portfolios | Individual `.dev`/`.com` sites with years of DR | Domain age, backlinks, case studies | Most ignore technical SEO + AI search; rarely bilingual |
| Agencies | Small Next.js / SEO shops | Team, content volume, DR | Higher prices, less personal; generic positioning |
| Marketplaces | Upwork / Toptal / Freelancer profiles | Massive DR, transactional intent capture | Commoditized, no brand control, fee-laden |
| Content sites / blogs | dev blogs ranking for "technical seo for X" | Topical authority | Not selling the service; we can convert the same query |

**Takeaway:** the open lane is **"developer who also does technical SEO + AI, with proof."** Few individuals combine all three credibly. The MBA + engineering combination is a genuine differentiator almost nobody else claims. Lead with the intersection, not any single service where DR-heavy incumbents win.

## Arena 2 — Farsi (Iran domestic) SERPs

| Competitor type | Strength | Weakness / our angle |
|---|---|---|
| Iranian web-design agencies (طراحی سایت) | Local brand, volume of pages, backlinks from .ir | Often weak technical SEO, slow sites, thin schema, poor CWV |
| Iranian SEO freelancers (متخصص سئو) | Local keyword targeting | Many lack real technical depth / international stack |
| AI/automation: nearly empty | — | First-mover advantage — almost no one positioning on practical LLM/automation in Persian |

**Takeaway:** This is the **underpriced arena.** Competitors rarely ship fast, schema-rich, Core-Web-Vitals-clean sites. His own site *is* the demo. AI/automation in Persian is near-greenfield. Expect faster ranking here and use it to build DR that also lifts `/en`.

## Arena 3 — Entity / brand space

- "Pouya Karimi" is not a rare name — disambiguation matters. Without strong Person schema + `sameAs`, the entity blurs with namesakes.
- **Action:** lock the entity with consistent name+title+photo+links across GitHub, LinkedIn, the Telegram channel (`t.me/cognitivedgebyp`), X, dev.to, and any guest posts. This is low-effort, high-return, and most freelancers neglect it.

## Strategic Recommendation

Don't fight DR-heavy incumbents head-on in English. Instead:
1. **Win the Farsi market first** (technical quality moat, near-empty AI niche) to build DR and revenue fast.
2. **Win the intersection in English** (dev × SEO × AI × MBA) via long-tail + proof, not broad head terms.
3. **Lock the entity** so brand and AI-citation queries resolve to him cleanly.
4. **Use the site as the portfolio piece** — its own speed, schema, and AI-search presence are the sales argument for the SEO service.
